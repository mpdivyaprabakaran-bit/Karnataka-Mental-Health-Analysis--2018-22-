# Karnataka-Mental-Health-Analysis 2018-22
**A longitudinal analysis of Psychiatric Morbidity and Resource Gaps in Karnataka (2018-2022)**

**Project Overview**: 
This project develops a unified "District Health Fact Sheet" for Karnataka by integrating multiple administrative and socio-demographic datasets. It tracks the prevalence of various mental health conditions across all districts over a four-year period to assess the state's mental health burden against its existing institutional capacity.

**Objectives**: 
Trend Identification: Visualize year-on-year growth or decline in specific mental health conditions (SMD, CMD, Substance Abuse). 
Resource Gap Analysis: Calculate Patient-to-Provider ratios by correlating district patient volumes with state-level professional counts (Psychiatrists, Psychologists, and Nurses). 
Socio-Economic Correlation: Investigate statistical relationships between district literacy rates, alcohol consumption, and reported mental health cases. 
Financial Efficiency Mapping: Assess "Budget per Patient" metrics by merging state budget approvals with total patient loads. 
Data Storytelling: Build an interactive Power BI dashboard to identify high-burden districts requiring urgent intervention. 

**Data Sources**: 
Data was integrated from data.gov.in across several dimensions: District-Level Patient Records (2018-2022). State Health Budgetary Approvals.Healthcare Infrastructure & Workforce. Socio-Demographic Indicators (NFHS-5).

**Tools & Technologies**: 
Excel: Data cleaning, transformation (Power Query), Pivot Tables, Correlation Analysis, and ANOVA. 
Power BI: Data modeling, DAX calculations (e.g., Staff-to-Patient ratio), and interactive dashboard creation. 

**Key Findings**: 
Case Growth: Total psychiatric patients in Karnataka rose from ~3.6 lakh in 2018 to over 11.2 lakh by 2022. 
Resource Strain: The current overall staffing ratio is approximately 1:9,608, with a budget allocation of only ₹70.36 per patient. 
Pandemic Impact: System growth dropped by 14% during the pandemic and later by 50%, indicating a significant "service gap" where patients could not access hospitals. 
High-Burden Districts: Raichur, Bellary, and Bangalore Urban consistently show the highest patient volumes and are strategic priorities for resource reallocation. 
Literacy/Alcohol Paradox: Weak correlations were found between literacy or alcohol consumption rates and reported hospital visits, suggesting that high patient counts may reflect better reporting or infrastructure rather than just higher prevalence. 

**Recommended Strategies**: 
Normalize Data: Move from analyzing "Total Sums" to "Patients per 100,000 residents" for more accurate crisis identification.
Targeted Investment: Focus on low-reported districts that may have hidden patient populations with limited access to care.
Standardized Reporting: Implement uniform data collection across the state to improve future analysis.
